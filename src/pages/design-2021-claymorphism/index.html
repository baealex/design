Claymorphism is neumorphism that went to art school. Same shadow engine — outer depth + inner highlights — but with color, bigger radii, and a personality that actually makes you smile.

The Dribbble shots that went viral were Blender renders, not CSS. The CSS version is a softer echo — it can't replicate that puffy 3D volume. And that's fine.

Because the real gift isn't the shadow formula. It's the warmth. The pastels. The roundness that says "don't worry, this is friendly." And there are specific contexts where that's exactly what an interface needs.

Technique

The Triple Shadow

Three shadows working together. An outer shadow lifts the element off the page. An inner dark shadow on the bottom-right simulates the clay curving away from the light. An inner light shadow on the top-left catches the highlight. Together they create the illusion of a soft, inflated 3D shape — using nothing but box-shadow and border-radius.

The Honest Truth

Neumorphism's Warmer Sibling

Let's be direct. Look at the shadow formula: outer + inset dark + inset light. That's neumorphism with an extra layer and color. The anti-pattern guides love to say "this isn't rebranded neumorphism" — but the CSS doesn't lie.

And that's fine. Evolution isn't plagiarism. Neumorphism was monochrome and cold — you couldn't tell a button from a wall. Claymorphism added pastel color (solving affordance) and warmth (adding personality). Same bones, different soul.

This palette is the real differentiator. Neumorphism was trapped in a single gray. Claymorphism escaped into a box of pastels — and that escape changed the emotional register from "design museum" to "kindergarten art room." For some products, that's exactly right.

Our Take

Where Clay Belongs

Not every interface should feel like a warm hug. A stock trading app in pastels would be terrifying. A legal document system made of clay would feel unserious.

But a children's learning app? A pet clinic? A wellness tracker? These products want warmth. They need approachability. They benefit from an interface that says "this is safe, this is friendly, come on in."

Claymorphism isn't a universal design system — it's a personality. Use it for products that have that personality.

The Rules

  • ✗ Clay everything — not every product has a playful personality
  • ✗ Pretending CSS clay equals Blender clay — those viral shots are 3D renders
  • ✗ Clay for authority contexts — finance, medical records, legal
  • ✗ Triple shadows on every element — the GPU budget is real
  • ✗ Elements too close together — overlapping shadows break the clay illusion and look floaty
  • ✓ Clay for warmth-first contexts — kids, pets, wellness, playful brands
  • ✓ The pastel palette is the real gift — warm, muted, non-threatening
  • ✓ Small elements first — buttons, badges, cards. Not full-page layouts
  • ✓ Neumorphism evolved, not replaced — same engine, warmer soul
