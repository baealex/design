Inspired by Neubrutalism

NEUBRUTALISM

Hard shadows and loud colors that refuse to whisper.
The problem isn't the volume. It's the lack of a volume knob.

Neubrutalism was a rebellion against polished sameness. Thick borders, solid shadows, clashing colors — every element stamped onto the page with zero apology.

The strength is obvious affordance — every button screams "click me." Hard edges and loud contrast make interactive elements impossible to miss. The risk is visual fatigue. When everything shouts at the same volume, the user's eyes have nowhere to rest — and no way to tell what matters.

We keep the boldness. We add a volume knob. Three levels of loudness — loud, medium, quiet — same border system, same personality, but now the interface can whisper too.

Technique

Hard Shadows

The signature move. A solid offset shadow with zero blur. Combined with a thick black border, every element looks like it's been stamped onto the page. No subtlety, no softness. The shadow doesn't simulate depth — it declares it. Move your cursor over the cards below and watch them lift.

Palette

Clashing Colors

Forget color harmony. Neubrutalism puts yellow next to pink, blue next to orange, and dares you to look away. Every element is its own announcement. The colors don't blend — they collide. And the black borders hold the whole thing together like a frame around chaos.

The Problem

When Everything Shouts

Five buttons. Five colors. Five shadows. Which one is the primary action? Which one matters? When every element is at maximum volume, the user hears noise — not a message. This is neubrutalism's fatal flaw: loudness without hierarchy is just chaos.

No hierarchy
Four buttons, all screaming. The user freezes.
Volume control
One shouts. One speaks. One whispers. The user knows.
The Fix

Volume Control

The border is the constant — every element gets the thick black stroke. That's what makes it neubrutalist. But shadow and color are volume knobs. Turn them up for primary actions, down for support. Three levels, one system.

The Rules

  • ✗ Every element at max volume — loudness without hierarchy is noise
  • ✗ Gradients, blurs, or soft shadows — that's another language entirely
  • ✗ Half-committed brutalism — if it's clean and polished, it's not this
  • ✗ Color as decoration — each color should signal a meaning, not just "loud"
  • ✓ The black border is the constant — that's what makes it neubrutalist
  • ✓ Shadow and color are volume knobs — turn up for primary, down for support
  • ✓ Three levels: loud (color + shadow), medium (white + shadow), quiet (border only)
  • ✓ One loud element per view — if everything's loud, nothing is
