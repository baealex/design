Inspired by Android Pie

Material 2

Google learned that a system needs room to breathe.
We keep the openness. Make sure it's not just emptiness.

Material Design 1 was a brilliant cage. Every app looked like Google. In 2018, Google I/O introduced Material Design 2 — the system that finally said "make it yours."

Shape theming. Google Sans for personality. Outlined inputs instead of underlined ones. White space as the primary surface. Bottom navigation replacing the hamburger menu. The era of "Google White" began.

The strength was breathing room — apps felt open, light, modern. The weakness was identity — when everything is white and airy, everything looks the same in a different way.

We keep the flexibility and the space. We make sure "clean" doesn't mean "empty."

Principle 01

Shape Theming

M2 introduced the idea that corners are a design decision. Not just one radius for everything — a system of shapes. Small components (4dp) for buttons and text fields. Medium (8dp) for cards and dialogs. Large (16dp) for bottom sheets and navigation drawers. For the first time, you could tune the personality of an entire app by adjusting three numbers. We keep this: shape as brand expression.

Principle 02

White Space as Surface

M1 used heavy shadows to separate content. M2 realized you could use white space instead. Cards got thinner borders. Backgrounds became lighter. The primary surface became #FFFFFF on #F8F9FA — almost nothing, but enough. Content breathes. We adopt this restraint: let space do the work shadows used to do.

Principle 03

Bottom Navigation

The hamburger menu was M1's biggest UX failure. Three lines in the corner, hiding the entire navigation behind a swipe nobody remembered to do. M2 moved primary destinations to the bottom of the screen — visible, reachable, always there. One thumb, all sections. We embrace this: put navigation where thumbs already are.

Visual Shift

Google Sans

Roboto was M1's workhorse — neutral, functional, everywhere. M2 introduced Google Sans for headlines and display text — rounder, friendlier, with just enough personality to feel like a brand without screaming it. Roboto stayed for body text. The pairing gave Google's products a warmth they never had. We borrow this: a personality font for headlines, a readable font for everything else.

Components

Contained, Outlined, Text

M2 softened the button hierarchy. Contained buttons with shadow for primary actions — same as M1's raised, but with Google Sans and configurable shape. Outlined for secondary. Text buttons for the lowest emphasis. The Extended FAB was new — a pill with an icon and a label, finally giving the FAB a voice instead of just a plus sign.

All Together

Google White App

White surfaces. Thin borders. Google Sans headlines. Tabs with a primary indicator. Cards with outlined borders. Bottom navigation visible at all times. An Extended FAB floating in the corner with a label. This is the app that lived in every pocket from 2018 to 2021 — clean, open, and unmistakably Google.

What This Is Not

  • ✗ White on white on white — space needs contrast to work
  • ✗ Material 1 with rounded corners — this is a different system
  • ✗ "Google Sans on everything" — it's for headlines only
  • ✗ Bottom nav with 7 items — three to five, no more
  • ✓ Shape theming as brand expression — three radii, one personality
  • ✓ White space as hierarchy — let content breathe
  • ✓ Bottom navigation — put destinations where thumbs are
  • ✓ Extended FAB — the floating action finally got a label
