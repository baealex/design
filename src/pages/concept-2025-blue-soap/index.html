Clean Interface

Soap

Clean as blue soap. Soft as a bubble.
Light as foam.

Most design systems need a dozen colors. Blue Soap asks: what if you only had one?

One hue — #1194ff — scaled by opacity. Buttons at 100%. Shadows at 15%. Borders at 12%. Background tint at 3%. The constraint forces every choice to be deliberate. No color fights another because there's only one color to begin with.

The metaphor is a bar of blue soap. The interface should feel like you just washed your hands — refreshing, clean, done.

Principle 01

One Hue, Every Layer

rgba(17, 148, 255) — this is the only color. Buttons, shadows, borders, alerts, focus rings, background tint. All the same RGB, just different opacity. Even shadows are blue, not black. That's what makes it feel cohesive instead of assembled.

Principle 02

Soap Film Surface

A soap bubble isn't opaque. It's a thin film that lets light through and bends what's behind it. That's backdrop-filter: blur plus rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.92). The surface isn't solid white — it's almost white, with a hint of whatever's underneath bleeding through. It feels lighter than a solid card because it is.

Principle 03

Everything Floats

Soap floats. Bubbles float. So does everything in this system. Hover lifts elements up and spreads their shadow. Nothing presses down. Corners are round because soap has no edges. Buttons are pills because bubbles don't have corners. The whole UI feels weightless — one tap and it might drift away.

What This Is Not

  • ✗ Blue text on everything — body text stays dark, blue is accent only
  • ✗ Black shadows on a blue palette — shadows match the hue
  • ✗ Opaque cards on a tinted background — surfaces are translucent film
  • ✗ Hover effects on static elements — only interactive things float
  • ✓ One hue at varying opacities builds every layer
  • ✓ Translucent surfaces with backdrop blur
  • ✓ Float-up hover — nothing presses down
  • ✓ Blue-tinted shadows that belong to the palette
