Inspired by Android 12

Material You

Your wallpaper became the design system.
We keep the personal color. Ensure the app still has its own voice.

Material You asked a radical question: what if the OS looked like you? Android 12 extracts a color palette from your wallpaper and paints the entire system with it. Buttons, toggles, notifications, even the keyboard — all tinted by one photo you chose.

The strength is emotional — your phone feels yours. The weakness is identity — when the OS recolors every app, apps lose their own brand. Everything becomes the same pastel blob in a different shade.

We keep the personal warmth. We protect the app's identity. Dynamic color is a starting point, not a straitjacket. The system suggests a palette. The app decides how much to use.

Principle 01

Dynamic Color

One image. One seed color. An entire tonal palette generated algorithmically. Google's "Monet" engine extracts the dominant hue from your wallpaper and builds a complete color system — primary, secondary, tertiary, plus neutral surface tones. The same app looks different on every phone. We embrace the idea: let users influence the palette. But we pick which surfaces accept dynamic color and which keep the brand.

/* M3 color roles — generated from a single seed */
--primary: tonalPalette(seed, 40);
--primary-container: tonalPalette(seed, 90);
--secondary: tonalPalette(seed, 40, desaturated);
--tertiary: tonalPalette(seed + 60°, 40);
/* One seed. Five tonal groups. Entire system colored. */
Principle 02

Color Roles

M3 doesn't just have "primary" and "accent" like Lollipop. Every color has a container pair — a muted background tint designed to hold content. Primary for key actions. Secondary for supporting elements. Tertiary for expressive accents. Each with an on-* text color guaranteed to have contrast. We use this system: containers for backgrounds, full tones only for the moments that matter.

Principle 03

Tonal Elevation

Lollipop used shadows to show depth. Material You uses color tint. Higher surfaces get more primary color mixed into them — they don't float above, they glow warmer. The shadow is nearly gone. Depth is communicated by surface tone, not by darkness underneath. We adopt this: subtle tonal shifts replace heavy shadows.

Visual Shift

From Paper to Pebble

Lollipop was paper — sharp, flat, rigid. Material You is a pebble — rounded, warm, organic. The radius went from 2px to 28px. FABs became rounded squares. Chips became pills. Cards became soft containers. The entire OS stopped feeling like a clipboard and started feeling like something you'd hold in your hand.

Components

Filled, Tonal, Outlined

M3 replaced "raised vs flat" with a richer hierarchy. Filled buttons use full primary — highest emphasis. Tonal buttons use the secondary container — medium emphasis, the new default. Outlined for low emphasis. The FAB is now a rounded square in the primary container color. Everything is a pill. Everything is soft.

All Together

Android 12 App

Rounded surfaces. Tonal elevation. Color containers. A bottom navigation bar with an active pill indicator. A FAB in primary-container, rounded square. No heavy shadows. No sharp edges. No uppercase buttons. This is what "personal" looks like as a system.

What This Is Not

  • ✗ Letting the OS recolor your brand out of existence
  • ✗ Pastel everything — some surfaces need to stay neutral
  • ✗ Material 1 with rounded corners — this is a new system
  • ✗ "It's the default M3 theme" — adapt it, don't copy it
  • ✓ Dynamic color as personal touch — not as total control
  • ✓ Tonal elevation — depth through color tint, not shadow
  • ✓ Container colors for comfort, full tones for emphasis
  • ✓ Rounded shapes that feel held, not clicked
