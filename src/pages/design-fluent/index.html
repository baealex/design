Inspired by Microsoft Fluent Design System

Fluent

Metro stripped everything away.
Fluent figured out what to bring back.

After Windows 8 went full flat, Microsoft had a problem. Users missed depth. They missed the feeling that UI elements existed in space, not just on a colored rectangle. But going back to Aero's glossy excess? That wasn't an option either.

So they built Fluent. Five pillars: Light, Depth, Motion, Material, Scale. The pitch was ambitious — a design system that would work from a phone screen to a HoloLens. The reality? Most of Windows still doesn't use all five. But the ideas that did ship? They're genuinely brilliant. A radial gradient following your cursor says more about hierarchy than a drop shadow ever could.

Principle 01

Acrylic Material

Acrylic is Fluent's frosted glass — backdrop-filter: blur() plus a noise texture overlay. That noise is doing more work than you think. Without it, blurred surfaces look like vaseline on a lens. With it, they feel like actual material — ground glass, not a rendering artifact. Microsoft's design team spent months tuning the noise frequency. We're borrowing their homework.

Principle 02

Reveal Highlight

This is the party trick. A radial-gradient that follows your cursor, illuminating borders and surfaces as you move. It's not decoration — it's light as interaction feedback. Your cursor becomes a flashlight, and the UI responds by showing you where things are, where boundaries exist, what you can click. No other design system has done this. Not before, not since.

Principle 03

Depth Without Weight

Fluent doesn't need heavy shadows to create spatial relationships. The system uses four elevation levels — Base, Card, Dialog, Flyout — each with progressively stronger shadows. The jump between levels is subtle. That's the point. Where Material Design screams "I'm floating at 8dp!", Fluent whispers. And somehow you still know exactly which layer is on top.

Base Mica background, no shadow
Card Content surface, 1px shadow
Dialog Focused context, soft shadow
Flyout Elevated action, deep shadow
All Together

Put It Together

Acrylic material + Reveal Highlight + depth layering. Three principles, one interface. The surface is frosted. The light follows you. The shadows know their place. It looks like something Microsoft dreamed up in 2017 but shipped in fragments. We're shipping it whole.

What This Is Not

  • ✗ Reveal Highlight on every element — it loses meaning when everything glows
  • ✗ Acrylic over dense content — frosted glass on a spreadsheet helps no one
  • ✗ Animating everything — motion sickness is a real accessibility concern
  • ✗ Five pillars on a page that only needs two
  • ✓ Light as interaction feedback — cursor reveals boundaries, not decoration
  • ✓ Material to establish hierarchy — frosted panels separate layers
  • ✓ Motion that connects states — enter here, exit there, stay oriented
  • ✓ Depth through restraint — four levels, not forty
