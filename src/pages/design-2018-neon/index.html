Neon design emerged from synthwave culture and cyberpunk aesthetics — the visual language of night cities, arcade halls, and imagined futures.

We take the electric intensity and channel it into interfaces where glow isn't decoration but information hierarchy.

Principle 01

Glow Is a Light Source

A neon tube doesn't just have color — it emits it. The trick is layered text-shadow: a tight core for intensity, a wide halo for ambient bleed. One layer says "I'm colored." Two layers say "I'm on."

Principle 02

Darkness Is the Canvas

Neon on a white background is just pink text. Neon on darkness is a light source. Same element, two completely different stories. The darkness isn't absence — it's the condition that makes neon real.

Principle 03

Color Temperature Tells Stories

Every neon sign on a street is a one-word story. Pink says come in. Blue says we're serious. Purple says stay a while. The best signs don't explain — they set a mood before you even read the words.
