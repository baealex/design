Inspired by Google Material Design

Material

Google asked: what if pixels were paper?
Good question. Complicated answer.

2014. Google's design was a dumpster fire. Android looked one way, Gmail another, Chrome OS like it was designed by a completely different company. Which, emotionally, it was.

Material Design was the grand unification: every surface is a sheet of paper. Paper has thickness. It casts shadows. You can stack it, cut it, slide it. Ink flows across it and settles where you touch.

The metaphor was genuinely brilliant. The execution got... religious. But we'll get to that.

Principle 01

Paper and Ink

Every surface has elevation. Elevation creates hierarchy. A card at 1dp is resting content. At 8dp, it's demanding your attention. Google specified this down to the pixel -- dialogs at 24dp, FABs at 6dp, navigation drawers at 16dp. The shadow is not decoration. It's a spatial coordinate system. Hover to see them rise.

Principle 02

The Ripple Effect

This was the stroke of genius. When you tap a surface, a ripple radiates from the exact point of contact. Not a generic highlight. Not a color change. A physics-based wave that says "yes, here, I felt that." It's the most honest piece of touch feedback ever designed. Click anywhere on these surfaces.

Principle 03

Meaningful Motion

In Material's world, nothing just appears. Nothing just vanishes. Elements expand from their origin, share from one surface to another, transform to reveal what was hidden. Motion is not eye candy -- it's a spatial explanation. Where did this come from? Where is it going? If your animation can't answer that, kill it. Click a card to see it expand.

All Together

Put It Together

Elevated surfaces. Ripple on touch. A FAB floating above the content plane. Three principles, one interface. It looks like a Google app because, well, this is the system that defined what a Google app looks like. The trick is stealing the principles without copying the skin.

What This Is Not

  • ✗ Following dp elevation tables like scripture
  • ✗ FAB on every screen -- not everything is a primary action
  • ✗ Ripple on non-interactive elements
  • ✗ "It's Material so it must be right"
  • ✓ Elevation as visual importance -- higher means louder
  • ✓ Touch feedback at the exact point of contact
  • ✓ Motion that explains spatial relationships
  • ✓ Principles over spec -- steal the thinking, not the stylesheet

Paper was the metaphor.
Don't let it become the cage.

Material gave Android a soul. Then it gave every Android app the same soul.
Use the physics. Ignore the dogma. Your app isn't a Google product.
